Storyteller Dice Roller

Instructions

This roller provides options for four of the most commonly used rolls in Werewolf the Apocalypse games.

The rolls follow the WtA 20th Anniversary Edition rules:

  • A roll of 10 gives 2 successes;
  • A roll of 1 cancels one success; and
  • A roll of 1 and no successes results in a botched roll (a critical failure).

Clicking on the buttons at the top of the roller to change between roll types. The UI will offer options specific to that roll.

Common controls

Roll options

  • Dice: enter the total number of dice to be rolled here. (Maximum: 30)
  • Difficulty: enter the roll’s difficulty. (Range: 2 to 10)
  • Difficulty modifier: you can enter the sum of any difficulty modifiers here. (Range: -8 to +8)
  • Bonus successes: enter the total number of automatic successes here (eg, bonus success from spending a Willpower point or bonus successes added to damage rolls from attack rolls).
  • Penalty botches: enter any success penalties here (eg, opponent’s successes when making an opposed roll).

Roll buttons

  • Roll: click to make the roll.
  • Re-roll: repeat the last-used roll, but without any automatic successes and botches.
  • Re-roll (adj): repeat the last-used roll, including automatic successes and botches.

Types of rolls

Standard

  • Use this for the standard ‘attribute plus ability’ and attack rolls, as well as willpower and gnosis checks.
  • If an ability’s specialisation applies to the roll, click the “Specialised” button. When this button is on, each 9 and 10 rolled will give two successes.
  • There are no other special options.
  • Results: Number of successes.

Damage

  • Unlike a standard roll, a damage roll cannot result in a botch, as 1s do not cancel successes. But 10s do not give two successes.
  • The Specialised button is removed, as ability specialisations don’t apply to damage rolls.
  • You can select to inflict bashing, lethal and aggravated damage using the drop-down menu. The damage type will be displayed in the roll’s result, but it doesn't make any difference to a roll.
  • Enter the number of bonus successes gained from the attack roll in the “Bonus successes” field, and the result of your opponent's soak roll in "penalty botches".
  • Results: Number of successes and damage type.

Rage

  • Use this when making rage rolls and frenzy checks.
  • The roll's difficulty is set automatically: click “crinos” if the garou is in crinos form, and then select the current moon phase, and the garou's auspice and rank from the drop-down menus.
  • Difficulty modifiers can still be applied.
  • Results: “calm”, “frenzy” or “In thrall of the Wyrm”.

Step sideways

  • The difficulty is based on the gauntlet level selected from the drop-down menu.
  • Difficulty modifiers can still be applied.
  • Results: “Success” and the time taken to enter the umbra; “fail”, or “botch”.
