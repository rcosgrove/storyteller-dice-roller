Storyteller Dice Roller

Backstory

The Storyteller Dice Roller is designed for use with the Werewolf the Apocalypse 20th Anniversary Edition role-playing game from Onyx Path Publishing.

I created it so I could make secret dice rolls while running Werewolf games.

This marks my first attempt at coding in Javascript. And it’s the biggest piece of coding I’ve done since I writing an Advanced Dungeons & Dragons character generator in BBC BASIC on my Acorn Electron, back when I was in high school, in dark days before broadband.

This roller has been released under Paradox’s 'Dark Pact' and a CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license. It’s not for sale, but tips to say thanks are always welcome.

I intend to develop the roller further, including the ability to save settings for rolls and an "attack" button that makes attack and damage rolls with one click, while also streamlining the code, but that could take some time. I decided to do this project five years back. Began it at the start of this month. And finished it four weeks later. But I have severe ADHD, and one way my ADHD presents is that my brain is full of ideas of projects I fully intend to do, yet never get round to doing. This is one of the times I did.

If you find this utility and use it, I hope you like it. If you want to send me some feedback, leave a comment on its GitHub repository or email me at the address on my GitHub profile.
